NIMBASIA ERP

Standard Operating Procedure — User Guide

Plain-language, step-by-step instructions for everyday use

For: Sales, Production, Dispatch, Purchase, Accounts and Admin teams

Document Version: 1.0    Date: 18 July 2026

About This Document

This guide explains, in simple language, how to do your daily work in the Nimbasia system. No technical knowledge is needed. Each section is a short procedure: what screen to open, what to fill in, which button to press, and what happens next.

The system follows one main journey:

Enquiry → Approval → Order → Production → Quality Check → Packing → Dispatch → Delivered

Alongside this journey, a purchasing loop keeps raw materials in stock: Purchase Order → Goods Receipt → Quality Check → Stock.

Who Does What (Roles)

Every person has one login and one role. You only see the screens your role needs. Pricing is hidden from everyone except Admin and Accounts.

RoleWhat they do in the system
AdminCan see and do everything. Also manages user accounts, master data (product and customer lists) and the activity log.
SalesLogs customer enquiries, approves or rejects them, creates and manages orders, and raises manual order requests.
ProductionRuns manufacturing batches: materials, equipment, testing, quality check, packing. Can raise purchase orders (without prices) and record stock adjustments.
DispatchShips completed orders: creates shipments, updates their status from packing to delivered, and enters dispatch dates for approved requests.
PurchaseRaises purchase orders — chooses the supplier, items and quantities. Never sees prices and cannot send a PO to the supplier.
AccountsAdds prices, tax, discount and freight to purchase orders, and sends the finished PO to the supplier.

Getting Started

Logging in

  1. Open the Nimbasia website in your browser (works on computer and phone).
  2. Enter your email and password, then press Sign In.
  3. You land on the screen for your role. The menu on the left (or the bottom bar on a phone) shows only the pages you can use.
Wrong password too many times? After 10 failed attempts the system blocks login for 15 minutes. Wait and try again.

Changing your password

  1. Click your name at the top right.
  2. Choose Change Password, enter the old and new password (at least 6 characters), and save.
  3. You stay logged in, but everyone else using your old password is logged out immediately.
Tip: If the screen suddenly returns to the login page, your session has expired. Just log in again — nothing is lost.

SOP 1 — Recording a Customer Enquiry (Sales)

When: a customer calls, messages or visits asking about a product.

  1. Open Enquiries and press New Enquiry.
  2. Fill in the enquiry date, how it came in (phone, WhatsApp, website, walk-in), and the company name. If the company already exists, pick it from the list so its details fill in automatically.
  3. Add one row per product: choose the product from the list, its grade, quantity and unit. You can add several products to one enquiry.
  4. Enter the expected price and currency, the customer's expected timeline, and who in your team owns this enquiry.
  5. If the customer needs it urgently, switch on the Urgent toggle — urgent enquiries jump the production queue later.
  6. Press Save. The system gives the enquiry a number and marks it Pending.
Sample follow-up: if a sample was sent, the list reminds you automatically after 12 days to follow up with the customer.

SOP 2 — Approving or Rejecting an Enquiry (Sales / Admin)

When: pending enquiries are waiting for a decision.

  1. Open Approval. You see every pending enquiry with its full details.
  2. To accept: press Approve and confirm. The enquiry becomes Accepted.
  3. To reject: press Reject. You must type a reason — the system will not let you reject without one.
Only a Pending enquiry can be approved or rejected. Once decided, the decision cannot be repeated.

SOP 3 — Creating and Managing Orders (Sales)

When: an enquiry has been accepted and the customer confirms the order.

  1. Open Orders. Create the order from the accepted enquiry — the product, quantity and customer details carry over automatically, and the order gets its own sales-order number.
  2. Check the delivery address. The order cannot go to production until city, pincode, state and country are all filled in. If something is missing, the screen tells you exactly what.
  3. While the order status is Created, you can still edit or delete it. Once production starts, it locks.
  4. When ready, press Move to Production. The order status changes and a production batch is created automatically for the factory team.

Manual order requests (no enquiry)

If a customer confirms an order directly and there is no enquiry on record:

  1. In Orders, create a Manual Order Request with the product, grade, quantity, packing and the full delivery address.
  2. The request waits for approval (Admin or Sales). Once approved and given a dispatch date, it becomes a real order just like the enquiry route.

SOP 4 — Running a Production Batch (Production)

When: an order has been moved to production and appears in your queue.

The queue

Open Production. Batches are listed urgent-first, then oldest-first. Each row shows the order, the product, how much has been produced so far, and the batch status (Not Started, Started, Partially Produced, Hold, Completed).

Working through a batch

Open a batch to see its overview and the eight steps. Complete them as the work happens on the floor:

StepWhat you record
1. Batch SetupBatch number, capacity, particle size, assigned staff, delivery date and produced quantity.
2. Raw MaterialsEach material used: item, vendor, grade, batch number and quantity per shift (A/B/C). The screen shows how much of each batch is available in stock.
3. AdditivesSame as raw materials, for additives.
4. CatalystSame as raw materials, for catalysts.
5. EquipmentEquipment checklist: name, ID and remarks.
6. Process ParametersTarget vs actual values, plus machine RPM settings.
7. Operation LogA running diary of the batch: time, operation, operator, remarks.
8. In-Process TestingShift sampling results (FFA, ash, moisture, appearance, melting point, etc.). For records only.
Substituting a material batch: if a planned batch of material runs short, use Substitute Batch on that row, give the reason and pick the replacement. The change is recorded in the batch history.
A batch status can only be changed twice. Plan status changes carefully — the system blocks a third change.

SOP 5 — Final Quality Check (Production)

When: the batch is completed (or partially produced) and finished goods need sign-off.

  1. Open the batch's QC Test Sheet.
  2. Enter the test rows: sample date, shift, who sampled, black particle, bulk density, sieve residue, who analysed, remarks.
  3. Choose the overall result — PASS, FAIL or PENDING — and the approver's name.
  4. Press Save.
What PASS does: the produced quantity is added to finished-goods stock and the order automatically becomes Ready for Dispatch. Nothing can be packed or shipped until QC passes.

SOP 6 — Packing (Production / Dispatch)

When: a QC-passed order needs to be packed for shipment.

  1. Open Packing. Each order card shows: ordered quantity, already packed, already dispatched, and what remains.
  2. Press Pack, enter the quantity packed, the packing material used, its quantity, who packed it, and remarks.
  3. Save. The packing history for the order updates.
The system will not let you pack more than was produced, or use more packing material than is in stock.

SOP 7 — Dispatching Orders (Dispatch)

When: packed goods are ready to leave the factory.

  1. Open Dispatch. The top shows orders ready to ship (with delivered and remaining amounts); below are the individual shipments.
  2. Press New Dispatch, choose the order, and enter the quantity — the system caps it at what is actually available to ship.
  3. Enter the dispatch date, tick whether packing is done, set the shipment status and add remarks. Save.
  4. As the shipment moves, update its status: Packing → Shipped → Delivered.
Delivered can only be set on the shipment that completes the full order quantity — it closes the order.

Entering dispatch dates for approved requests

Open Dispatch Date. This queue lists accepted enquiries and approved manual requests that do not have an order yet. Entering a dispatch date converts each into a real order automatically, with the address filled in.

SOP 8 — Purchasing Raw Materials (Purchase / Production / Accounts / Admin)

Purchasing is deliberately split between two roles so that ordering and pricing stay separate.

Step A — Raise the purchase order (Purchase or Production)

  1. Open Purchase Orders and press New PO.
  2. Choose the supplier — existing suppliers fill in their contact, GST and address automatically; a new name creates a new supplier.
  3. Fill in category, bill-to and ship-to plant, order date, expected delivery date and department.
  4. Add item rows: description, category, unit, grade and quantity. You will not see price fields — that is normal.
  5. Save. The PO stays in Draft.

Step B — Price and send (Accounts or Admin)

  1. Open the draft PO. Accounts sees the price, tax, currency, discount and freight fields.
  2. Fill in all pricing and save.
  3. Press Send to Supplier — this button only works once the PO is fully priced.
  4. Use Print for a clean, letterhead-ready copy of the PO.

SOP 9 — Receiving Goods (GRN)

When: material ordered on a PO arrives at the gate.

  1. Open GRNs and press New GRN (Admin).
  2. Pick the purchase order the delivery belongs to — only POs still awaiting material appear.
  3. Enter the received date, who received it, vehicle reference and warehouse location, and the quantity received against each ordered item.
  4. Save. The GRN is in Draft.
  5. Record the quality test on the GRN: sampling rows, the overall result, and the approver (the approver must be a different person from the analyst).
  6. When the result is PASS, press Confirm. Only then is the material added to stock.
Never press Confirm before QC has passed — Confirm is what puts the material into usable inventory.

SOP 10 — Checking and Adjusting Stock

  1. Open Raw Materials. The table shows stock on hand for every item and batch — lowest stock first — plus summary tiles at the top.
  2. Use the Stock Register tab to see any date's opening stock, what came in, what was consumed per shift, and the closing stock.
  3. To correct a physical difference, use Manual Adjustment: choose IN or OUT, the quantity, and a reason. The reason is mandatory — every adjustment is traceable.
Never try to fix stock by editing past records. Always use a Manual Adjustment with a clear reason, so the history stays truthful.

SOP 11 — Admin Tasks

Managing users

  1. Open Users. Press Add, enter the name, email, role and a starting password (at least 6 characters).
  2. Tell the person to change their password on first login.

Master data

Open Master Data to maintain the dropdown lists everyone else picks from: products, persons, supervisors, enquiry modes, units, and the customer list (with Excel import). Supplier Data does the same for suppliers.

Activity log

Open Activity Log to see who did what and when: every important action is recorded with the person, time, and what changed. Use the search and filters to investigate any question about a record's history.

Golden Rules

#Rule
1An order can only be created from an accepted enquiry.
2Production cannot start until the order has a complete delivery address (city, pincode, state, country).
3Nothing ships until the final quality check is a PASS.
4You can never dispatch more than has been produced and packed.
5Rejections always need a written reason — enquiries and purchase orders alike.
6Prices on purchase orders are Accounts' job; ordering quantities is Purchase's job. Neither does the other's.
7Goods enter stock only through a confirmed, QC-passed GRN; corrections only through reasoned manual adjustments.
8Change your password after your first login, and never share logins.

Need Help?

  • Forgot your password / locked out: contact your Admin — they can set a new password for you.
  • A record looks wrong: do not delete or overwrite it. Note the record number and tell your Admin; the activity log shows its full history.
  • The site is not loading: try refreshing once; if it persists, inform your Admin — do not keep retrying a form submission, as it may create duplicates.
